# Bounded, risk-gated escalation instead of a flat cycle cap

When the Chairman's synthesis has a load-bearing gap, `/council` can loop back through research → Stage 3 revision → re-synthesis (an Escalation Cycle), capped at 4 per run — but escalation isn't automatic. The Chairman must first rate the gap on Likelihood (how likely resolving it is to change the answer) and Exposure (how costly being wrong about it would be), each Low/Medium/High, and only escalate when the combination genuinely warrants the extra cost; a low-stakes gap is finalized with an explicit caveat instead.

We considered a flat single-cycle cap (simpler, but a genuinely high-stakes gap only gets one shot at resolution) and unbounded looping (risks never converging, unbounded cost). The risk-gated design spends extra cycles only where the Chairman's own judgment says it's worth it, while the hard cap of 4 guarantees termination regardless of how the risk assessment goes.
